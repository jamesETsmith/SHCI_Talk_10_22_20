Quantum Chemistry

Image from pixabay

Goal: Describe the behavior of electrons and nuclei.

\[ \hat{H}_e\Psi = E\Psi \]

\( \hat{H}_e : \) how the electrons interact with other electrons and nuclei

\( \Psi : \) contains all information about the electrons (it's what we're solving for)

Solving this problem exactly is expensive

The majority of my research aims to reduce this cost

  • To study these problems, we use quantum chemistry where our goal is to describe the behavior of electrons and nuclei full stop.
  • In order to do this, we need to solve the time independent schrodinger equation.
  • Here \( \hat{H}_e \) is the electronic Hamiltonian that describes exactly how everything in our system interacts and we know it.
  • \(\Psi\) is the wave function and it's what we're solving for. It contains all the information about the system.
  • Solving this equation exactly is really challenging and expensive to do.
  • For any fans of complexity theory out there, this problem is NP-hard, aka really hard.
  • The majority of my research aims to reduce this cost without giving up too much accuracy.
